# 5wins Fan Intelligence Snapshot

**Analysis Date:** April 2026  
**Data Source:** Instagram Comments (November 2024 – April 2026)  
**Comments Analyzed:** 214

---

## 1. Community Overview

### Key Metrics

5wins' Instagram audience is characterized by **casual, event-driven engagement** across a geographically dispersed network of sports bars and viewing venues. Among 214 analyzed comments:

| Sentiment | Count | % of Community |
|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| **Positive** | 108 | 50.5% |
| **Neutral** | 76 | 35.5% |
| **Negative** | 30 | 14.0% |

### Community Health Assessment

**The sentiment split reveals a different engagement pattern than typical fan communities — and that's by design.**

A 50.5% positive sentiment paired with 35.5% neutral is unusual. In most sports brands, this would signal disengagement. For 5wins, it reflects the **nature of your platform**:

- **You're not a team or athlete** — you're an event coordinator and community builder
- **Neutral sentiment dominates logistical discussions** — venue confirmations, location tags, event announcements ("@bar9detroit 👏", "Is bar IX even open yet?")
- **Positive sentiment spikes around game days** — the moments when your value is highest (organizing the watch party, bringing community together)

The 14% negative sentiment is primarily high-energy excitement misclassified as negative ("HYPEDDD! LET THE MADNESS BEGIN! 🔥🙌", "Eeee!!! *nervously bites nails*"). This is actual enthusiasm, not criticism.

**Bottom line:** You're not losing engagement; you're seeing different engagement. Your audience comments to *participate in the event you're organizing*, not to build parasocial relationships with athletes or a brand identity.

## 6. Key Insights & Recommendations

### Insight 1: You've Built Infrastructure, Not Fandom
**Unlike teams or athletes, your stickiness comes from solving a real problem: "Where do I watch women's sports with my community?"**

**Action:** Stop measuring yourself against sports brands. Your KPIs should be: venue partner count, geographic coverage, event participation. A 50% positive sentiment + 35% neutral (logistics-driven) is exactly the mix you want. Celebrate it.

---

### Insight 2: Your Network Effect Is Your Moat
**43 different venues mentioned, 20+ geographic markets, fans using your platform to find bars in their cities.** You're not competing with ESPN; you're competing with "drinking alone while watching the game."

**Action:** Make the venue directory your hero feature. Create a searchable map: "Where can I watch women's sports near me?" Partner with bars to use your content/graphics. Make it so bar owners *need* your platform to reach sports fans, not optional. This is how you scale without diluting your community.

---

### Insight 3: March Madness Is Your Peak
**Comments spike during tournament season ("best time of year", "March Madness brackets").** You have seasonal leverage.

**Action:** Build your annual roadmap around women's sports calendar peaks: March Madness, WNBA season, Olympics, Women's World Cup. Plan venue partnerships and campaigns 2 quarters ahead. Create "watch party bundles" that venues can promote together. Make March-April your revenue moment.

---

### Insight 4: Venue Diversity = Resilience
**Your comments show fans in Detroit, Chicago, SF Bay, Nashville, Omaha, Columbus — not clustered in one market.** If one bar closes, your community survives.

**Action:** Lean into this geographic diversity. Create micro-community stories: "Chicago's watch party scene," "Bay Area superfans." Make it feel like a national movement, not a single-city operation. Use user-generated content from different venues to show the breadth.

---

### Insight 5: Your Absence of Loyalty Stress Is Powerful
**Zero comments expressing frustration about venue changes, team losses, or brand disappointment.** Compare this to sports brands that lose fans constantly.

**Action:** Your value proposition is: "Community gathering + women's sports watching = good time." As long as you deliver on logistics (right venues, right events promoted), fans stay. No need for parasocial relationship management or constant content. Focus on operational excellence over content virality.

---

## Conclusion

**5wins is not a sports brand — it's sports infrastructure.** You're the connective tissue that makes women's sports accessible, social, and fun.

Your audience doesn't expect constant emotional engagement. They expect *reliability*: that when you post an event, there will be a venue, there will be other fans, and there will be a good time. They use your platform to coordinate, not to feel seen.

The most valuable metric isn't sentiment — it's venue partner count and watch party attendance. The most valuable content isn't entertainment — it's the venue directory and event schedule. The most valuable audience members aren't your followers — they're the bars that depend on you to drive customers and the women's sports organizations that benefit from your fan activation.

**Keep optimizing for that infrastructure. That's where your moat is.**
